Calories

What are calories?

Calories are a unit of energy also seen as (kcal) on food packaging. Since they are a form of energy when we consume food our bodies breaks the food down during digestion into a form of energy called ATP (adenosine triphosphate). That energy has to be stored somewhere in the body, as energy cannot be deleted, nor created as defined by the laws of physics.

So why are calories so important?

Calories dictate what our weight does. Whether your weight goes up, down or stays the same is dictated by calories.

Calories for building muscle

Calories are paramount for gaining muscle. Without a calorie surplus it is very hard to gain muscle at all, because an excess of energy is required to put towards building muscle. So during a fat loss phase you won't build muscle unless you are extremely new to training or taking anabolic steroids, which assist muscle growth and fat loss. This is dictated by a signalling process in the body called mTOR, which requires energy (calories) to be activated. If you are eating too few the body would prioritise keeping us alive, instead of building muscle.
